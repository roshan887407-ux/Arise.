6. Protocol Feature — Permissions and Scope

Protocol exists for one reason: to help you reclaim your time from apps designed to steal it. The average person spends 4–7 hours per day on their phone — not by accident, but by design.

To function, Protocol requires sensitive Android permissions. Here is exactly what each one does — and critically, what it never does:

Permission What It Does What It NEVER Does
Usage Access Reads how much time you spend in each app. Shown only to you. Never uploaded. Never leaves your device. We cannot see it.
Notifications Delivers focus session alerts and reminders you configure. Never used for unsolicited marketing.

All Protocol permissions are opt-in. You can revoke any permission at any time through Android Settings. Revoking a permission only disables that specific Protocol feature — it does not affect the rest of the App.

Protocol data — screen time, focus sessions — is stored only on your device. It is never uploaded to any server, never analyzed by us, never shared with anyone.
